Landmarks of History and Faith

Sitabuldi Fort

Perched atop a twin hillock, Sitabuldi Fort is a poignant relic from the British era. It was the site of the pivotal Battle of Sitabuldi in 1817 and now houses an Indian Army memorial. History enthusiasts can visit the cell where Mahatma Gandhi was imprisoned during India's struggle for independence.

Markanda

There are no places named "Narkanda" in or near Nagpur; it appears there is a misunderstanding, as Narkanda is a hill station in Himachal Pradesh, approximately 60 km from Shimla. For places and attractions in Nagpur, popular sites include Deekshabhoomi Stupa, the Zero Mile Stone, and various lakes like Futala and Ambazari

Ramtek Temple

Ramtek is a historical place near Nagpur famous for its Ram Temple, built by the Maratha ruler Raghuji Bhonsale in the 18th century. It's a significant pilgrimage site rooted in the epic story of Lord Rama, who is believed to have rested here during his exile. Beyond the temple, historical sites in the Ramtek area include the Nagardhan Fort and the Kapoor Baoli stepwell.

Dragon Palace

The Dragon Palace is a Buddhist temple in Kamptee, near Nagpur, not a historical site, though it is notable for its unique architecture and Indo-Japanese friendship, completed in 1999. It is known for its stunning design, a large sandalwood statue of Lord Buddha, peaceful gardens, and fountains.
